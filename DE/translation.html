Translate-a-thon at the University of Michigan!

Every year, the Language Resource Center at the University of Michigan holds a translation marathon called Translate-a-thon. Every year, volunteer translators come together to work on translating projects that have direct impacts on the community. For example, the first time I attended, the German team worked on transcribing and translating a handwritten letter from a WWII Theresienstadt Concentration Camp survivor. What an exciting project! I have since participated three times and have had so much fun every time. Check out the Language Resource Center's Translate-a-thon website! I am featured in one of their videos.

Canon Translation Review Submission

In 2014, I submitted an original German-to-English translation of "Moni malt ein Meisterwerk," a short story by Michael Ende, to the Canon Translation Review, which is a literary magazine dedicated to publishing translations by undergraduate and graduate students at the University of Michigan. Although my translation was never published, it was certainly a fun project to work on! Michael Ende's stories are always a joy to read, and this short story had never been translated before. It's about a creative girl named Moni who paints a picture with the help of her older friend, the narrator. See my submission here!
